Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 2026

🔒 BananaPlayer collects zero data. Period.

Overview

BananaPlayer ("the App") is developed by Benjamin Naro. This privacy policy explains how we handle your information — the short version is: we don't collect any.

Data Collection

BananaPlayer does not:

  • Collect any personal information
  • Track your usage or behavior
  • Store any data on external servers
  • Require you to create an account
  • Use analytics, crash reporting, or tracking tools
  • Share any data with third parties

Local Storage

All app data — including your playlists, favorites, viewing history, and settings — is stored locally on your device. This data never leaves your device and is not accessible to us or anyone else.

Third-Party Servers

BananaPlayer connects to third-party servers only when you provide a playlist URL (M3U/M3U8) or Xtream server credentials. These connections are initiated by you and go directly from your device to the server you specified.

We have no control over and assume no responsibility for the content, availability, or privacy practices of these third-party services. You are solely responsible for the content you access through the App.

Apple App Store

BananaPlayer is distributed through the Apple App Store. Apple may collect certain data as described in Apple's Privacy Policy. This is outside our control.

Subscriptions

If you purchase a subscription, the transaction is handled entirely by Apple through the App Store. We do not receive or store any payment information, credit card details, or Apple ID data.

Children's Privacy

BananaPlayer does not knowingly collect any data from anyone, including children under 13. Since we collect no data at all, there is nothing to address under COPPA or similar regulations.

Changes to This Policy

If we ever change this privacy policy, we will update this page and note the revision date above. Given that we collect zero data, significant changes are unlikely.
